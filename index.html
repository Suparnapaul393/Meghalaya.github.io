The Myth

Krem Marai is associated with the fabled 'Pah Syntiew', the ancestral queen mother of the Syiem(chief) of Mylliem(a state in bygone days) and Khyrim (another state). It is said that: In olden days a rumour got abroad that there was a woman in a cave called Marai, which is situated near the present village Pomlakrai, at the source of the river Umiew or Umiam. She was a beautifil damsel. Many trieed to catch her, but they could not, owing to the narrowness of the cave. There came however, a ceratin very clever man who went to entice her by showing her a flower called "U liew-jalyngkleng". The damsel then cam eout to snatch the flower, but the man went on holding back his hand until she came out in a more open place, when he seized her. He then brought her to his house and carefully tended her.

That damsel was called "Ka Pah Syntiew",the flower-lured one, beacause the man caught her by coaxing and enticing her with a flower... a wonderful woman, who, it seemed very clear, was the daughter of the god "god Shillong"[in Khasi: U Blei Shillong].

What?

Krem Marai is a cave situated ona granite hillock being formed largely by clefts in the rock. It is approximately 30m in length.

Where?

Krem Marai is located near the village of Nongkrem and to the east of Shillong Peak in Meghalaya,North-East India.

Why?

the Khyrim Syiemship Tribe perform the annual religious rites dedicated to the founder ancestor and the ancestress of the state. The Nongkrem festival is celebrated as a tribute.
